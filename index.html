The Rights of Canadian Women in History


Exhibit Framework

The purpose of this website is to propose a new museum exhibit and allow visitors to see what kind of content the exhibit would consist of.

The rights of women in Canada went through major reform beginning in 1878 and continuing into the present day. Canadian women began their journey to equality with very little rights. They were not even regarded as “persons” under Canadian law. They were unable to own property, vote, pursue a higher education or have a say over their own reproductive organs.

First Wave Feminism

The first wave of feminism in Canada is one of great importance. Navigation through each decade of the lives of Canadian women and the early struggles to be seen as equals would be a valuable and enriching learning experience.

The framework of this museum exhibit is intended to be a decade-by-decade walkthrough of the average woman’s life in a highly visual and interactive environment. Each section of the exhibit would be an inside look – detailing the strict limitations and frustrations of Canadian women on a daily basis. The exhibit is mean to make visitors familiar with the pressures placed upon women of the past and to generate interest about the women who stepped up to demand change.

I The influence of women in the late 1800s to early 1900s resulted in enormous changes for human rights in Canada. This resulted in the empowerment of many women and led to further movements in the 1960s and onwards. The Famous Five, a well-known group of brave and relentless women were at the forefront of the movements. They will be a feature of the exhibit, and as such, have their own section on this webpage.
