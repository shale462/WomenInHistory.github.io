Education and Careers

Women were becoming more and more interested in earning a proper education in order to establish themselves in the labour force. In 1881, there was a 75.6% rise in girls attending school compared to 1842. Women were beginning to enter the workforce, but only in “female professions”, which consisted of jobs like teaching – the first of the woman-centric professions.


Voting Rights - A Glimmer

It was during this decade and the next that women began to receive municipal voting rights. It was only granted to those who were spinsters or widowers in Ontario in 1884. Voting was deemed to be a privilege, not a right and women were dismissed when they tried to bring up the issue of voting as they were seen as too emotional to handle politics.
